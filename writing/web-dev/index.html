The Necessity of Website Accessibility

With our society becoming more reliant on the web, website accessibility has grown into a necessity that benefits all. Website accessibility is especially paramount during the current COVID-19 pandemic which has brought more people than ever to the web for a range of needs; from online schooling to online shopping to socializing with loved ones. Website accessibility can be defined as a concept wherein websites are designed, developed and modified so that everybody, regardless of ability, can access and interact with them. Unfortunately, there are reportedly “millions of websites which do not follow accessibility guidelines (Crespo).” Presently, the responsibility of accessible web design is mostly in the hands of web developers. Although some avoid it out of the idea that these guidelines require more effort or knowledge than they can muster, the benefits of accessible web design far outweigh the perceived inconveniences of creating it.

The statistics around this issue are disheartening. As of 2019 studies analyzing websites for accessible web design found that 70 percent of websites surveyed contained “accessibility blocks” and only one percent of the top million homepages on the web meet accessibility standards, wrote Arielle Pardes for WIRED. This is all despite the fact that around 7 million people have a visual disability in the United States, and more than that have a myriad of other disabilities that affect their access to the web. A lot of the issues encountered aren’t even difficult to remedy--like screen readers being faced with vague labels for elements like “image1.png” or “link (Pardes).” While some web design accessibility is more nuanced, involves more knowledge, or can be more complex, simple accessibility problems like these somehow persist. What is Search Engine Optimization (SEO)?

It appears to be in the name already but you may be wondering--what exactly is “Search Engine Optimization?” The most basic definition is that it is a methodology to improve your site’s visibility in search engine results. The value in SEO is its ability to attract more visitors to your website (“What is SEO / Search Engine Optimization?”). Today search engines are the most prominent way that people find websites, whether they represent businesses or artists or something in between, and are therefore invaluable to those owning or maintaining websites.

To better understand SEO, it's important to better understand how search engines operate. Search engines (think Google, Bing, Duckduckgo, etc) create massive indexes of websites using bots, called crawlers, that collect information from sites and use that information to include them in the aforementioned index. Algorithms then analyze the pages within the index, utilizing hundreds of factors, to figure out the ranking of pages in search results for a given query. The factors in question are typically determined based on estimates of how well a website or page can give the search engine user what they want to find (“What is SEO / Search Engine Optimization?”). The design of the website should be clean and inviting, just like your restaurant. This means simplicity; simple layout, clear navigation, and accessibility.
